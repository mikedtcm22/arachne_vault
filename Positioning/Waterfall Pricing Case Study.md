# Waterfall Pricing Case Study

Up: [[Arachne Intelligence]] · Supports [[Finance Leadership Briefcase]]

The worked precedent for the **cost-driver → value-driver** pricing move — the same move behind the ResFrac strategic narrative and the [[Finance-as-a-Service]] pricing thesis.

## Context
Waterfall: a mobile-marketing software company (mid-2010s), ~30 people, ~$10M revenue. Michael was Director of Finance (2012–2017); the pricing rollout (~2015) was diagnosed and promoted by him, signed off by CEO/CFO.

## The problem
Pricing had two levers — a monthly software fee plus a **per-message fee**. The per-message side was a race to the bottom (near-telco-scale competitors; Enterprise clients driving message prices down for volume). The underlying flaw: pricing was triggered by a **cost/consumption** activity, not a value-creating one.

## The redesign
Removed message count from pricing entirely; replaced the two-tier structure with a pure software license scaling on **Audience Size** (subscriber-list size). Viable partly because a regulatory cap (~10 messages/number/month) bounded per-customer cost exposure. Customers now model spend on a metric they *want* to grow; the message shifted from "you're penalized for sending more" to "we both win as your audience grows." (Honest caveat: implicit max-message pricing never fully left — audience × legal cap — but it stopped being the explicit driver.)

## Ownership & results
Michael owned the full pricing redesign **and** the sales-comp redesign — a gross-margin component in commissions plus a Finance↔Sales "deal desk" for upcoming-deal communication. Phased over ~1 year (new contracts immediately; existing at renewal). Gross margins moved from roughly high-50s to roughly low-70s by acquisition (~2 years later). *(GM figures from memory, hedged.)*
